// Technical Round
Most Asked Technical Questions
DSA Explain the difference between BFS and DFS. When would you use each? ▾
BFS explores nodes level by level using a queue — ideal for shortest path problems. DFS goes deep using a stack/recursion — better for cycle detection, topological sort, and maze pathfinding. Use BFS when the solution is close to root; DFS when memory is constrained or exploring all paths.
OOP What are the 4 pillars of OOP? Give real-world examples. ▾
Encapsulation (hiding state — bank balance), Abstraction (exposing only needed interface — car ignition), Inheritance (Dog extends Animal), Polymorphism (shape.draw() for Circle vs Square). All four reduce complexity and improve maintainability.
System Design a URL shortener like bit.ly. Walk through your architecture. ▾
Key components: Hash function to generate 6-char shortcode, NoSQL DB (Cassandra/Redis) for key-value mapping, CDN for fast redirects, load balancer, analytics service. Handle collisions by appending incrementing suffix. For scale: shard by shortcode prefix, cache hot URLs in Redis, use consistent hashing for distribution.
DB Explain ACID properties. What happens if a transaction fails mid-way? ▾
Atomicity (all or nothing), Consistency (DB stays valid), Isolation (transactions don't interfere), Durability (committed data survives crashes). If a transaction fails mid-way, the rollback mechanism undoes all partial changes using the undo log, restoring the DB to its prior consistent state.
OS What is a deadlock? What are the necessary conditions for it to occur? ▾
Deadlock occurs when two or more processes wait on each other indefinitely. Four Coffman conditions: Mutual Exclusion, Hold and Wait, No Preemption, Circular Wait. Prevent by breaking any one of these four conditions.
// Behavioral Round
STAR Framework Questions
STAR Tell me about a time you failed and what you learned from it. ▾
Structure: Situation — context. Task — your responsibility. Action — what you did (own it, don't deflect). Result — outcome + what you learned. Choose a real failure with a genuine lesson. End on growth — show self-awareness.
STAR Describe a situation where you had to work with a difficult team member. ▾
Structure: Situation — context without blaming. Task — what outcome needed to happen. Action — focus on what YOU did (1-on-1 conversation, finding common ground). Result — how it improved collaboration. Never badmouth the other person. Show empathy and communication skills.
STAR Give an example of when you showed leadership without a formal title. ▾
Structure: Choose a project or team situation where you took initiative. Action — how you stepped up (organized, delegated, motivated). Result — measurable outcome. Shows you don't need authority to lead — a critical signal for senior roles.
// HR Round
Common HR Questions
HR Why do you want to join our company specifically? ▾
Reference 2–3 specific things — a product, a recent initiative, their culture value. Formula: "I'm drawn to [Company] because of [specific reason 1] and [reason 2]. My experience in [skill] aligns with [their need]." Avoid generic answers like "great culture" or "big brand."
HR What is your expected CTC and how did you arrive at that number? ▾
Research market rates (Glassdoor, AmbitionBox, LinkedIn Salary). Give a range, not a fixed number. "Based on my research and [X years / specific skills], I'm targeting ₹[X]–₹[Y] LPA. I'm open to discussion once I understand the full compensation structure." Always ask about ESOPs, bonus, and benefits.

Interview Execution Tips
TIP 01
Think Out Loud
Interviewers evaluate process, not just answers. Narrate your reasoning — "I'm thinking X because…" shows structured thinking even when you're stuck.
TIP 02
Clarify Before Diving
Spend 2 minutes clarifying scope before solving. Ask about edge cases, input constraints, expected output. Shows you think like an engineer, not just a coder.
TIP 03
Start with Brute Force
Always state the naive solution first with its complexity. Then optimize. This shows systematic improvement and communicates you understand tradeoffs.
TIP 04
Ask Questions Back
At the end, ask thoughtful questions about the team, tech stack, current challenges. "What does success look like in 90 days?" shows genuine interest.
